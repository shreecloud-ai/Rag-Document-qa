Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the simulation of human intelligence in machines. It enables systems to learn, reason, and make decisions.

Machine Learning (ML) is a subset of AI that allows computers to learn from data without being explicitly programmed. Common algorithms include linear regression, decision trees, and neural networks.

Deep Learning is a specialized branch of ML that uses neural networks with multiple layers. It is widely used in image recognition, natural language processing, and speech recognition.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique that combines information retrieval with text generation. It retrieves relevant documents and uses them as context to generate accurate answers.

Vector databases like FAISS and Pinecone are used to store embeddings and perform similarity search. Embeddings are numerical representations of text that capture semantic meaning.

Reranking improves the quality of retrieved results by reordering them based on relevance. Models like cross-encoders are commonly used for reranking.

Large Language Models (LLMs) such as GPT are capable of generating human-like text. They are used in chatbots, summarization, and code generation.

Evaluation of RAG systems includes metrics like accuracy, relevance, and faithfulness. Confidence scores help determine how reliable an answer is.

RAG Technology
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is an AI technique that improves LLM responses by retrieving relevant document chunks before generation.
It reduces hallucinations and grounds answers in real data.
Key components: chunking, embeddings (Sentence-BERT), vector search (FAISS), keyword search (BM25), and hybrid retrieval.
Reranking with cross-encoders further improves relevance.
